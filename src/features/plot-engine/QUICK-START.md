## üöÄ Your First Plot Analysis

### Step 1: Navigate to Plot Engine

1. Open your project
2. Look for **"Plot Engine"** in the sidebar or navigation menu
3. Click to open the Plot Engine dashboard

You'll see five tabs:

- **Overview** - Main analysis view
- **Story Arc** - Visualizations
- **Characters** - Relationship networks
- **Plot Holes** - Detected issues
- **Generator** - AI plot creation

---

### Step 2: Run Your First Analysis

1. Make sure you're on the **Overview** tab
2. Click the **"Analyze Plot"** button
3. Wait for the analysis to complete (typically 10-30 seconds)

**What happens during analysis:**

- üìä Story structure is analyzed
- üîç Plot holes are detected
- üë• Character relationships are mapped
- üìà Tension and pacing are calculated
- ‚≠ê Quality score is computed

---

### Step 3: Review Your Results

#### Quality Score

At the top, you'll see your **overall quality score** (0-100):

- **90-100** üéâ Excellent! Your plot is solid
- **70-89** ‚úÖ Good, with minor issues to address
- **50-69** ‚ö†Ô∏è Needs improvement
- **Below 50** üö® Significant issues detected

#### Story Summary

You'll see a summary like:

> "Found 2 plot holes (1 major, 1 minor). Story has strong pacing with a clear
> three-act structure."

---

### Step 4: Explore Visualizations

Click on the **"Story Arc"** tab to see:

#### Tension Curve

A line chart showing emotional intensity across chapters:

- **Rising**: Building tension ‚ÜóÔ∏è
- **Flat**: Plateau (might need work) ‚Üí
- **Falling**: Resolution ‚ÜòÔ∏è

**Tip:** A good story has peaks and valleys, not a flat line!

#### Pacing Chart

A bar chart showing how fast your story moves:

- **High bars**: Fast-paced, action-heavy
- **Low bars**: Slower, contemplative
- **Mixed**: Good variety

**Tip:** Vary your pacing to keep readers engaged!

---

### Step 5: Check Plot Holes

Click on the **"Plot Holes"** tab:

#### What You'll See

Each plot hole shows:

- **Type**: Timeline, character inconsistency, logic, etc.
- **Severity**: Critical (red) ‚Üí Minor (blue)
- **Description**: What the issue is
- **Affected Chapters**: Where it appears
- **Suggested Fix**: How to resolve it

#### Example

```
üî¥ CRITICAL: Timeline Contradiction
Description: In Chapter 2, Sarah is 25. In Chapter 5 (one year later), she's 30.
Affected: Chapters 2, 5
Fix: Adjust Sarah's age in Chapter 5 to 26, or change the time jump to 5 years.
```

#### Filtering

Use the filters to focus on:

- **Severity**: Critical, Major, Moderate, Minor
- **Type**: Timeline, Character, Logic, etc.
- **Search**: Find specific issues

---

### Step 6: Explore Character Relationships

Click on the **"Characters"** tab:

#### Relationship Network

You'll see:

- **Nodes**: Your characters (size = importance)
- **Lines**: Relationships (thickness = strength)
- **Colors**: Relationship types
  - ‚ù§Ô∏è Red: Romantic
  - üü¢ Green: Friend
  - üî¥ Red (thick): Enemy
  - üü° Yellow: Family
  - üîµ Blue: Rival

#### Relationship Cards

Click on a relationship to see:

- **Type**: Friend, enemy, romantic, etc.
- **Strength**: 0-10 rating
- **Evolution**: How it changed over time
- **Key Moments**: Important scenes

---

### Step 7: Generate Plot Ideas (Optional)

Click on the **"Generator"** tab:

#### Fill Out the Form

1. **Premise**: Brief story idea
   - Example: "A detective investigates a series of mysterious disappearances"

2. **Genre**: Choose your genre
   - Mystery, Fantasy, Romance, Sci-Fi, etc.

3. **Target Length**: Number of chapters
   - Short: 10-15
   - Medium: 15-25
   - Long: 25-40

4. **Act Count**: Story structure
   - 3 acts (most common)
   - 5 acts (complex stories)

5. **Conflict Type**: Main conflict
   - External (vs. antagonist)
   - Internal (vs. self)
   - Both

6. **Tone**: Story mood
   - Light, Dark, Dramatic, Humorous, etc.

#### Generate & Review

1. Click **"Generate Plot"**
2. Wait 20-30 seconds for AI to create suggestions
3. Review the generated structure:
   - **Acts**: Major story divisions
   - **Plot Points**: Key turning points
   - **Scenes**: Suggested scene ideas
   - **Conflicts**: Tension sources
   - **Resolutions**: How conflicts resolve

4. **Use as inspiration** or **save to your project**

## üí° Tips for Best Results

### Writing Tips

1. **Write Complete Chapters**
   - At least 1000 words each
   - Include dialogue and descriptions
   - Show character interactions

2. **Be Consistent**
   - Use character names consistently
   - Track dates and timelines
   - Maintain character voices

3. **Develop Characters**
   - Add character profiles
   - Define traits and goals
   - Show growth and change

### Analysis Tips

1. **Analyze Periodically**
   - After writing 5+ chapters
   - Before major revisions
   - When stuck on plot

2. **Don't Over-Analyze**
   - Not every "plot hole" is real
   - Use as guidance, not rules
   - Trust your creative instincts

3. **Iterate**
   - Fix critical issues first
   - Re-analyze after changes
   - Track improvement over time

## üìä Understanding Your Results

### Story Arc Types

The analyzer identifies your story structure:

#### Three-Act Structure (Most Common)

- **Act 1 (Setup)**: Introduce characters, world, conflict
- **Act 2 (Confrontation)**: Rising tension, obstacles, midpoint twist
- **Act 3 (Resolution)**: Climax, falling action, resolution

#### Five-Act Structure

- **Act 1**: Exposition
- **Act 2**: Rising Action
- **Act 3**: Climax
- **Act 4**: Falling Action
- **Act 5**: Resolution

#### Hero's Journey

- Ordinary World ‚Üí Call to Adventure ‚Üí Trials ‚Üí Ordeal ‚Üí Return

### Plot Hole Severities

#### üî¥ Critical

Fix immediately - breaks story logic

- Timeline contradictions (2 years = 5 years old?)
- Character identity confusion
- Major logical impossibilities

#### üü† Major

Address soon - hurts story quality

- Character inconsistencies (personality changes)
- Unresolved major plot threads
- Significant pacing issues

#### üü° Moderate

Consider fixing - minor problems

- Small inconsistencies
- Weak foreshadowing
- Minor timeline issues

#### üîµ Minor

Optional - nitpicks

- Very small inconsistencies
- Stylistic concerns
- Minor pacing variations

## üéØ Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Low Quality Score

**What it means:** Multiple issues detected

**What to do:**

1. Go to Plot Holes tab
2. Sort by severity (Critical first)
3. Fix critical issues
4. Re-analyze
5. Repeat until score improves

---

### Scenario 2: Flat Tension Curve

**What it means:** Story lacks ups and downs

**What to do:**

1. Add conflict in low-tension chapters
2. Build toward a climax
3. Create plot twists
4. Vary emotional intensity

---

### Scenario 3: No Character Relationships

**What it means:** Characters not defined or not interacting

**What to do:**

1. Go to Characters section
2. Add character profiles
3. Write scenes with character interactions
4. Re-analyze

---

### Scenario 4: Pacing Too Fast/Slow

**What it means:** Story moves unevenly

**What to do:**

- **Too fast**: Add description, reflection, world-building
- **Too slow**: Cut unnecessary details, add action, increase stakes

## üÜò Troubleshooting

### "Analyze" Button Disabled

**Possible reasons:**

- No chapters written yet
- Project not loaded
- Previous analysis still running

**Solution:** Wait for project to load, ensure chapters exist

---

### Analysis Takes Too Long

**Possible reasons:**

- Many chapters (20+)
- Slow internet connection
- AI provider issues

**Solution:** Be patient (can take up to 1 minute), check connection

---

### Weird Plot Holes Detected

**Why:** AI isn't perfect, may misinterpret

**Solution:** Use judgment - dismiss false positives, focus on real issues

---

### Character Graph Empty

**Possible reasons:**

- No characters defined
- Characters don't appear in text
- Names used inconsistently

**Solution:** Add character profiles, ensure names match exactly
